
# 🌍 **Travel Pattern Risk Engine — Full Implementation Blueprint**

This subsystem analyzes **global and domestic travel patterns** to detect:

- Pre‑incident reconnaissance  
- Foreign intelligence officer movement  
- High‑risk travel by sensitive personnel  
- Travel anomalies correlated with cyber or physical events  
- Multi‑country movement patterns indicating coordination  
- Sudden shifts in travel behavior linked to geopolitical tension  

It fuses **travel telemetry**, **geopolitical context**, **identity risk**, and **cross‑vector intelligence**.

---

# 1. **System Architecture Overview**

### Core Components
- **Travel Telemetry Collector** — ingests flight, hotel, border, and corporate travel data  
- **Identity & Role Context Engine** — maps travel to sensitive roles and clearances  
- **Route Pattern Analyzer** — detects unusual or high‑risk travel paths  
- **Geopolitical Risk Engine** — overlays regional tension and diplomatic context  
- **Anomaly Detection Engine** — identifies deviations from normal travel behavior  
- **Threat Scenario Engine** — models known travel‑linked threat patterns  
- **Fusion Output Layer** — pushes alerts to dashboards & correlation engines

---

# 3. **Travel Feature Engineering**

### Core Feature Categories
- **Route Risk Score** — based on geopolitical tension, intelligence presence, sanctions  
- **Travel Entropy** — unpredictability of travel patterns  
- **Identity Sensitivity Score** — role‑based risk weighting  
- **Temporal Drift** — changes in travel frequency or timing  
- **Cross‑Vector Correlation** — travel aligned with cyber/physical anomalies  
- **Peer‑Group Deviation** — divergence from normal travel for similar roles  

### Feature Storage
- Stored in time‑series DB  
- Indexed by identity, region, country, and facility  
- Supports sliding‑window analysis (7d, 30d, 90d, 1y)  

---

# 4. **Anomaly Detection Engine**

### ML Models
- LSTM sequence models for travel pattern drift  
- Isolation Forest for route anomalies  
- Gaussian Mixture Models for clustering travel behaviors  
- Bayesian changepoint detection for sudden shifts  

### Rule‑Based Models
- Travel to sanctioned or high‑risk regions  
- Travel coinciding with cyber or physical anomalies  
- Sensitive personnel traveling without authorization  
- Multi‑country hops within short time windows  
- Travel patterns matching known intelligence TTPs  

### Output
- Severity score  
- Confidence score  
- Contributing features  
- Recommended actions
