To align the metadata precisely with how an advanced threat actor or counterintelligence framework operates, we must shift from simple keyword tags to Generalized Operational Conditions. These conditions describe the fundamental state of the operation, stripping out specific technical jargon so that they can apply universally to any comparable attack vector.
------------------------------
## Part 1: Updated Generalized Conditions Schema
The metadata arrays have been restructured into three definitive behavioral states:

* operational_viability_conditions: Conditions that prove the operation is proceeding completely unimpeded.
* compromise_and_failure_conditions: The generalized state of the operation when discovered, blocked, or decoupled.
* strategic_vulnerabilities_exposed: The exact vulnerability category exposed when a failure occurs.

{
  "tactic_id": "string",
  "metric_id": "string",
  "metric_name": "string",
  "operational_viability_conditions": [
    "generalized_states indicating uninhibited infrastructure or human movement"
  ],
  "compromise_and_failure_conditions": [
    "generalized_states indicating systematic detection, neutralisation, or collapse"
  ],
  "strategic_vulnerabilities_exposed": [
    "the structural blind spot revealed by a failure condition"
  ]
}
