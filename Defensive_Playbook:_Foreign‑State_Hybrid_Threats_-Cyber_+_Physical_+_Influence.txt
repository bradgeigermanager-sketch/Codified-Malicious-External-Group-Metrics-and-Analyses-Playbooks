# 🛡️ **Full Defensive Playbook: Foreign‑State Hybrid Threats (Cyber + Physical + Influence)**  
*(Defensive doctrine for protecting organizations, infrastructure, and leadership from state‑aligned APT and destabilization campaigns)*

---

## 🧭 **0. Purpose & Scope**  
This playbook provides a **unified defensive framework** against foreign‑state hybrid operations that combine:

- **Cyber intrusions**  
- **Physical infiltration**  
- **Human intelligence (HUMINT) placement**  
- **Supply‑chain compromise**  
- **Disinformation & destabilization campaigns**  
- **Leadership disruption operations**

It is designed for:

- national‑level security teams  
- critical infrastructure operators  
- enterprise security directors  
- fusion centers  
- emergency management leadership  

---

# 1. 🧩 Threat Landscape Overview  
Foreign‑state hybrid operations typically follow a **multi‑vector doctrine**:

- **Hybrid Operations** — simultaneous cyber, physical, and influence activity  
- **APT Cyber Campaigns** — long‑term, stealthy intrusions  
- **Human Intelligence Threats** — diplomatic cover, business fronts, NOC agents  
- **Critical Infrastructure Targeting** — power, water, telecom, logistics  
- **Political Destabilization** — disinfo, infiltration, pressure campaigns  
- **Insider Threat Operations** — recruited or coerced employees  

These operations are designed to **erode trust, disrupt leadership, and weaken institutional capacity**.

---

# 2. 🛠️ Early‑Warning Indicators  
A robust defensive posture begins with **indicators of preparatory activity**.

### 2.1 Diplomatic & HUMINT Indicators  
- **Anomalous diplomatic activity**  
- Unusual embassy staffing changes  
- Foreign officials contacting mid‑level personnel  
- “Cultural exchange” or “business delegation” visits with unclear purpose  

### 2.2 Cyber Reconnaissance Indicators  
- **Cyber reconnaissance indicators**  
- Credential stuffing attempts  
- Repeated scanning of ICS/OT ports  
- Targeted phishing of leadership or IT admins  

### 2.3 Influence & Destabilization Indicators  
- **Detecting disinformation campaigns**  
- Sudden narrative shifts across multiple platforms  
- Bot‑amplified outrage cycles  
- Leaked documents appearing in foreign media first  

### 2.4 Physical & Supply Chain Indicators  
- **Supply chain anomaly detection**  
- Unexpected subcontractors  
- Hardware with undocumented components  
- Foreign nationals attempting facility access

# 5. 🧵 Incident Response Framework  
A unified response to hybrid threats follows five phases.

### Phase 1 — Detection  
- Alerts from cyber, physical, or influence systems  
- Cross‑vector correlation  

### Phase 2 — Triage  
- Severity scoring  
- Identify affected domains  
- Notify leadership  

### Phase 3 — Containment  
- Network segmentation  
- Physical lockdown  
- Narrative suppression (defensive, factual communication)  

### Phase 4 — Eradication  
- Remove malware  
- Remove compromised access  
- Remove physical access vectors  

### Phase 5 — Recovery  
- Restore systems  
- Rebuild trust  
- Conduct after‑action review
